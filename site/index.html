Another path to big, human impact

A narrative for ambitious operators who want to lead real companies, steward jobs, and modernize the economic backbone — without having to start from zero.

I’ve got a bone to pick with venture capitalists: They’ve done their jobs too well. Around the world, ambitious people - entrepreneurs, founder-wannabes, inspired creatives and operators who want to take the leap from their day jobs and become their own boss - those people's best option is to start a startup. There doesn’t seem to be any similarly ambitious way to use our brain cells to their fullest and get rewarded for making impact in the business world.

We’ve created so much startup folklore: founding a unicorn, inventing a cornerstone product, and making boatloads of money

I’ve followed that path myself - I quit my job to sign up for more than one startup accelerator; I moved to Silicon Valley for Y Combinator and loved every moment of the founder journey. I don’t regret any of it for a second. Did I make it big? Not really. Did I learn a ton? Absolutely. So - I’m all for startups and accelerators, but I want there to be at least one other enticing path for ambitious entrepreneurs who are seeking impact.

My issue is that we’ve created a single-minded system in our VC-fueled, hockey-stick-seeking mindset. It shouldn’t be “founder vs normie”, and I want to challenge the mindset that starting a company is fundamentally more impactful - or difficult - or sexy - than turning around an existing business and making it an incredibly awesome place to work.

Really, I want to challenge the VCs who compete for talent … One that prioritizes making the world better by turning struggling legacy businesses into amazing places to work with positive cash flow and really good bosses.
