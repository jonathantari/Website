Manifesto

Another path to big, human impact

A narrative for ambitious operators who want to lead real companies, steward jobs, and modernize the economic backbone — without having to start from zero.

I think we can do better for these founders-to-be.
A prompt to rethink the only path we glorify.

There is this problem in the world. Ambitious people - entrepreneurs, founder-wannabes, people who are inspired to take the leap from their day jobs and become their own boss - those people's best option is to start a startup. That’s great, but should that be the only option? The path (coming up with a product, trying to be competitive in the market, and then ultimately building and leading the company around that venture) may not really be what they're excited about. It might be that they want to lead a company and drive enormous impact, but the successful startup is in the way of them getting there. Shouldn't they at least be able to consider an alternative? Don’t get me wrong - much of our innovation and many amazing products come from this path. But not every ambitious CEO-to-be needs to build a new product from scratch in order to have the impact that they want. And not every startup-to-be should exist in the world. It can be obvious when the product is a means to an end - simply a niche someone found which they want to build a business around. And there's nothing wrong with that - it is pretty easy to see how it happens. But I think we could do better.

Imagine yourself in these shoes. Maybe you even have been in these shoes (I have). You're starting this startup journey from scratch: You make a Venn diagram with the following areas: Where you have any expertise or an “unfair advantage,” where there’s a marketable product to sell, and where the competition hasn’t already figured everything out. If you’re VC-backed, then you’re also looking for a market that can hockey-stick, where the TAM is large enough to sustain a high-growth unicorn-to-be. It makes sense that you may find yourself building a company around a niche you don't have much passion for other than the fact that you may have a company that can survive at all.

What if we offered them something different than the startup path? Bear with me for a minute with this thought experiment.

What if they skipped the part where they build a reasonably arbitrary product which doesn’t end up having wings, and they then pivot to a modernized-version-of-legacy-for-X business? What if they skipped the part where they try to take down the competition and win their customers over (though maybe slowly) through hard-won sales, by building everything from the ground up, and driving their competitors to keep up or die out?

What if they skipped that part and inserted themselves into the same legacy company in the first place, to save it from the competition that will inevitably come?
Use the existing backbone — and upgrade it.

What if they skipped that part and inserted themselves into the same legacy company in the first place, to save it from the competition that will inevitably come? What if they could benefit from the legacy company's hidden gems and valuable assets, and replace all the parts that need to be fixed? What if they really put their mind to setting this company on a fast track path to profitability? Ruthlessly prioritizing and modernizing and optimizing in ways the original business owners weren't able to, and expanded on the business's success, while rebuilding and refocusing the losses? That kind of venture would be fast paced, super high impact, and might even look similar to the startup journey, fast forward a few years. Might be exactly what the ~founder~ leader-to-be is looking for.

Maybe this could even be good in the greater scheme of things? What if in doing so they cut out the inefficiency loss that comes with starting everything from scratch and building everything from the ground up? Incorporating a new business, building a new website, scrappy product testing, hiring people, training and onboarding, finding an office space - the list goes on and on. There is so much waste that goes into doing this over and over again (which is why PE firms love to buy up smaller companies and consolidate). On a macroeconomic scale, reducing this overhead translates into more effort and capital that can be deployed into making the company cash-flow positive. And by the way - they could still do those parts that they want to, they would just get to do it by choice (and if it’s valuable to the business), not just because it has to be done.

What if someone trained that leader-to-be to actually be a really good people leader?
Cash flow is fuel. People leadership is the point.

What if someone trained that leader-to-be to actually be a really good people leader? What if they prioritized "making great places to work" the end goal of this business succeeding? People in these jobs have likely been suffering if the company is not doing well. Translating the company’s success to the employees, and modernizing the workplace, working culture, people leadership, and benefits takes investment, but that could be *precisely* the purpose of becoming cash-flow positive. Now that sounds like a valiant goal for the new CEO of the company. It makes the entire mission feel a bit more human, doesn’t it? And because their incentives should be aligned, they would have a revenue share with the company’s profit. Huge payouts should be the goal too.

And the kicker: What if someone did this whole thing in a way that was repeatable? What if they did it in the same way that a venture studio makes their founder and investment process scalable? What if we (now is when we get to the we part) built an institution that consistently set up the leader-to-be for success - in any vertical, with a funnel of high-likelihood businesses to turn around, an engine for recruiting top talent and enticing them with great terms and a powerful mission, a partnership with the local municipality and innovation/economic funding to kick-start the flywheel and help drive impact? What if we could take this turnaround studio and drag and drop it into any local municipality that wants to infuse their local economy? Now that’s the level of impact I’m hoping for.

→

Drive the economic backbone of the local economy and save jobs

Make people’s jobs even better and create a wave of incredible, thoughtful managers in our communities

Do this at scale, in a drag-and-drop way in any city.
