Problem

We turned ambition into a single path. Start a startup or feel behind.

Ambition gets crowded into founding

Talented people want impact. The default path pushes them to invent a product, raise, and build a company from scratch. That works for some. It also creates waste when the target already has a viable product and customers.

Legacy businesses are under-served

These companies hold real jobs, real knowledge, and real assets. Many stall on execution, not vision. They need operators who can diagnose, set constraints, and ship visible wins without burning the place down.

Incentives shape the narrative

We celebrate founders and unicorn lore. We underwrite accelerators and hero stories. We do not reward the operator who gets a stalled plant back to cash positive and shares that value with the people on the floor.

The gap

There is no scaled, reputable path that matches ambitious operators with legacy companies and gives them the mandate and support to turn things around. That is what Turnaround exists to build.

Solution

Embed a vetted operator with a one year mandate and strict guardrails.

Vet and match

We maintain a curated pool of operators mapped by capabilities and references. We match them with owners who offer real authority and a clean mandate.

Prep and plan

A three month cohort focuses on diagnostics, cash clarity, throughput, and alignment. The output is a first 30-60-90 that everyone can understand.

Execute and show wins

Weekly metrics, ruthless scope cuts, and a shipping cadence. Confidence returns when the work is visible and useful.

Institution

A repeatable program that aligns incentives and protects the core business.

Guardrails

Clear decision rights. Explicit constraints. Review cadence with advisors who have shipped real programs. Owners get line of sight without micromanagement.

Aligned upside

Operator incentives tie to durable cash flow and employee outcomes. The north star is simple. Cash positive and fair distribution of gains.

Lightweight tools

Dashboards that track leading indicators. Operating rhythms that fit on one page. Process that helps people do their work, not fight software.

Nonprofit backbone

We are building an institution with mission first design. The objective is healthy companies that treat people well. The model follows that truth.

Impact

What this does at scale

Local economies

Stabilized mid sized firms keep skills, apprenticeships, and supplier networks alive. These are the quiet engines of a region.

Better managers

Programs that teach constraint setting and throughput create leaders who build teams that ship. That culture persists after the cohort ends.

Fair distribution

When cash improves, employees benefit. That is the agreement. It is visible and simple to audit.

Less waste

Fewer me too products. More repair and renewal of useful businesses. Capital gets more productive in the places where people work.
